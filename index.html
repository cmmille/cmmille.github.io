Chris Miller

plant biologist, software developer, wildlife photographer

Plant Biology

I am interested in the evolutionary relationships in plants. My M.S. work studied the reproductive barriers in wild tomato species. I am experienced with a wide array of molecular techniques including RNAseq, Western Blots, Flow Cytometry, and more.

Software Development

Computers have been an important part of my life since I first started modding computer games as a kid. In school, I first learned Python, R, and the Unix command line to process data and run statistics. Three years ago, I took on the role of frontend developer for my lab's MS Access Database. I recently earned my Certificate of Computer Programming. I am always trying to improve my skills and am currently enrolled in different Web Development courses through Udemy.

I am comfortable working with a wide variety of platforms and languages from Visual Basic to Java (including Android and Spring) to Web Development (HTML/CSS/JavaScript).

See my work on Github.

Wildlife Photography

My fiancee and I are avid bird watchers. With a pair of binoculars and my camera in hand, we love hiking around in search of new feathery friends.

I currently shoot with a Canon Rebel T7i using the Sigma 150-600mm f/5.6-6.3 Contemporary lens.
